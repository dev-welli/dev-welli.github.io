T8: JavaScript's Scope

What 'scope' is, and how it works in JavaScript.

Scope determines the accessbility or visibility of variables. In JavaScript there are two types of scope:

  • Local scope
  • Global scope
As the name implies, variable(s) with local scope can only be seen locally, inside the function where the variables are declared.
Conversely, global variables can be seen/used from outside the function.

Probably the easiest way to explain it is like this:
Let's say I have a garage and my car is currently parked inside it. Only someone (who has no previous knowldge of the car) inside the garage
will know the details of the car e.g. type, color etc

If the car is parked outside then anybody can know the type of the car without the need to entering the garage.
